Seasonal Cleanup Services

Thorough spring and fall cleanups that prepare your lawn and landscape for the season ahead, from leaf removal and dethatching to full winter readiness.

The transition between seasons is a critical time for your lawn and landscape. A proper spring cleanup clears away the debris of winter and sets your turf and plants up for a strong, healthy growing season. A thorough fall cleanup removes leaves and organic matter before winter, preventing the mold, disease, and turf damage that can occur when organic debris is left to sit on grass through cold, wet months. Powerline Maintenance has provided professional seasonal cleanup services to residential and commercial clients throughout Clemson, Anderson, and Easley, SC since 2010.

Our spring cleanups are designed to refresh every part of your landscape after winter dormancy. We remove accumulated leaves, dead plant material, and storm debris from lawn areas and beds, dethatch where needed to allow light and air back into the turf, and assess the overall condition of the lawn and plants going into the growing season. This early-season attention makes a measurable difference in how well your lawn and plantings perform through spring and summer.

Our fall cleanups prepare your property for winter and set the stage for a faster, more vigorous green-up the following spring. Leaf removal is central to this work — a heavy layer of leaves left on the lawn through winter can smother grass, block sunlight, and create the moist conditions that fungal disease thrives in. We also cut back perennials, clear out annual beds, and make any final preparations to protect your landscape through the colder months ahead.

What's Included

  • Complete leaf removal from lawn areas, beds, and hardscapes
  • Dethatching to remove accumulated dead grass layer and improve turf health
  • Debris removal including sticks, seed pods, and winter storm litter
  • Cutback of ornamental grasses, dead perennial foliage, and spent annuals
  • Final mowing and edging of the season in fall, or first of the season in spring
  • Bed clearing and preparation for new mulch or seasonal plantings
  • Inspection of lawn and landscape for winter damage or emerging issues
  • Gutter clearing available as an add-on service during fall cleanup
  • All debris hauled away or composted — no cleanup left behind for you

Why Seasonal Cleanups Matter

Seasonal cleanups are not simply about appearances — they have a direct impact on the health and vigor of your lawn going into and coming out of the dormant season. Thatch buildup, if left unaddressed, creates a barrier that prevents water, fertilizer, and oxygen from reaching the root zone. Leaves left to decompose on turf create acidic, anaerobic conditions that kill grass and invite disease. Heading into spring with a clean, clear landscape means your fertilization applications, overseeding, and irrigation all work more effectively from the very first application. Powerline Maintenance takes seasonal preparation seriously, because the work done at the transitions sets the tone for everything that follows.
