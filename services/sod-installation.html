Sod Installation & Grading Services

Transform bare dirt, patchy areas, or uneven yards into a lush, level lawn — ready to enjoy in days, not months.

Whether you are building a new home, renovating a damaged yard, or replacing an old lawn that has seen better days, professional sod installation gives you an instant, beautiful result. At Powerline Maintenance, we handle the full process — from grading and soil preparation to sod delivery, installation, and initial watering guidance — so you get a healthy, established lawn without the guesswork.

Proper grading is the foundation of every great lawn. Without it, you end up with standing water, erosion, and uneven growth. Our crew reshapes your yard to ensure positive drainage away from your home and structures, creating a smooth, level surface that looks great and functions properly for years to come.

We source high-quality, locally grown sod varieties suited to Upstate South Carolina's climate, including Bermuda, Zoysia, and Fescue. Our team installs sod with tight seams, proper staggering, and firm contact with the prepared soil — the details that make the difference between sod that thrives and sod that fails.

What's Included

  • Site assessment and grading plan for proper drainage
  • Land grading with laser-guided and skid steer equipment
  • Soil preparation including topsoil, amendments, and starter fertilizer
  • Premium sod selection matched to your property's sun and shade conditions
  • Professional sod installation with tight seams and proper staggering
  • Initial rolling for firm soil-to-sod contact
  • Watering schedule and care instructions for establishment
  • Cleanup of all debris and materials from the job site

When You Need Sod Installation

Sod installation is the right choice when you want a finished lawn quickly. Common situations include new construction where the yard is bare dirt, yards damaged by drought or disease, areas torn up by construction or utility work, or simply replacing an old, worn-out lawn with fresh turf. Unlike seeding, sod gives you a usable lawn in weeks rather than months, with far less risk of washout, weeds, or patchy growth.

Why Grading Matters

Even the best sod will struggle on poorly graded land. Standing water drowns grass roots, and slopes directed toward your foundation can cause serious water damage over time. Our grading work ensures water flows where it should — away from structures and toward proper drainage areas. We also address low spots, ruts, and uneven terrain so your finished lawn is smooth, level, and easy to maintain.
